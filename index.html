What We Measure

Five behavioral dimensions from survey constructs — grounded in behavioral finance literature

📣

Market Information (MI)

MI1–MI3: Reliance on market news, analyst reports, and financial media in investment decision-making.

Shefrin & Statman (1985)
🐑

Herding Behavior (HB)

HB1–HB4: Tendency to follow the crowd — buying because others buy, selling because others sell.

Bikhchandani et al. (1992)
🧮

Heuristics (He)

He1–He4: Cognitive shortcuts — anchoring, availability bias, representativeness, overconfidence.

Kahneman & Tversky (1974)
📚

Stock Knowledge (SK)

SK1–SK3: Financial literacy — understanding of stocks, market mechanics, financial ratios.

Lusardi & Mitchell (2011)
🎯

Attitude (AT)

AT1–AT6: Investment attitude — risk tolerance, willingness to invest, propensity toward financial decision-making.

Ajzen (1991) — TPB
